Best Homemade Brownie Recipe

Brownies are thought to originally come from the US, the name was derived from the mythical characters in children's stories of the time. One story suggests that brownies were created at the Palmer house in Chicago in 1892.The first recipe for brownies comes from the 1896 Boston Cooking School Cookbook for molasses cakes baked in individual pans. Chocolate brownies didn't actually become popular until the 1920s, when chocolate was more available to everyone. Early recipes were made with butter, eggs, sugar, flour, melted chocolate, and nuts. Even though the recipe was published 100 years ago it's barely changed.

These homemade brownies will be super fudgy, moist, with crispy edges and tops. For this recipe you won't need more than basic cooking necessities with the exception of unsweetened cocoa powder which you would probably have to go shopping for. These brownies take around an hour to make, including set up and clean up too. super fudgy, moist, and chocolaty, with crispy edges and crackly tops.

Ingredients

  • 2 Eggs
  • Water
  • Powdered Sugar
  • Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
  • Oil (Canola or Olive preferred)

Instructions

  1. First mix dry and wet ingredients into separate bowls.
  2. Combine the sugar, flour, powdered sugar, cocoa powder, chocolate chips, and salt.
  3. Whisk together the eggs, olive oil, and water.
  4. Combine the wet and dry ingredients. Sprinkle the dry mixture over the wet one, and fold until just combined. The brownie batter should be pretty thick.
  5. Pour the batter into an 8 by 8 inch baking pan lined with parchment paper. Use a spatula to spread it to all four sides of the pan and to smooth the top. The mixture should be very thick.
  6. Now it's time to bake. Put the pan in a 325-degree oven and bake for 45 minutes.
  7. Let the brownies to cool completely before slicing and serving.

Tips

  • Use room temperature eggs
  • Use melted butter
  • Whip the eggs and sugar until fluffy
  • Add flavor enhancers like vanilla extract or salt
